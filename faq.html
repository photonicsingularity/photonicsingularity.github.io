Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Is attendance compulsory for all lunch meetings?

A. While we do not require members to attend all meetings, everything we do during lunch meetings will be extremely helpful either towards both model UN conferences and life skills, and it isn't a very good idea to go to a conference without practice or preparation. However, it is a good idea to contact one of our execs through email to let us know you won't be showing up to a lunch meeting. In the end, the most important thing is just to enjoy your experience at CBMUN!

 

Q. Do we need to go to conferences?

A. Conferences are entirely optional, and will cost money most of the time. However, like lunch meetings, they are very educational and fun!

 

Q. Do we need to prepare for lunch meetings and conferences? If so, what should we prepare for?

A. Yes! You should be preparing for both lunch meetings and conferences, especially conferences. After you have received your country assignments, and there has been a topic assigned for a lunch discussion, it is a good idea to do 10-15 mins or research on both the situation surrounding the topic itself and your country's position on the topic. As for conferences, many conferences request you to write a position paper to make sure you have some knowledge on the subject. However, it is a good idea to do further research and draft an opening speech, operative clauses, or whole working papers.

 

Q. If there are limited spots for a conference, how will the participants be chosen?

A. Usually first come first serve, unless the conference requires experienced delegates.

 

Q. What do we have to wear to conferences or lunch meetings?

A. While lunch meetings do not have a dress code and are usually causal, but conferences usually have a western business attire dress code.
