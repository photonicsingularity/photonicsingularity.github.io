Colonel By Model United Nations

"The United Nations is our one great hope for a peaceful and free world."

- Ralph Bunche (1904-1971)

Model UN is a simulation of the United Nations, where everyone represents a delegate of a specific country in an UN assembly. The goal of a model UN committee, like the UN, is to pass resolutions that will resolve the issues being debated, while promoting the understanding and discussion of global affairs and politics.

What is the United Nations?

The United Nations, composed of 193 member countries, was founded in 1945 in order to promote peace, security, and economic development. Guided by the purposes and principles contained in the United Nations Charter, its mission is to encourage international cooperation within multiple committees and legislative bodies.

Who are we?

We are Colonel By Secondary School's model United Nations club, and we meet every Tuesday at lunch in room 124, all year! Every meeting, we will either be simulating a United Nations committee, practicing our public speaking skills, learning about the United Nations itself, or preparing for one of the many annual model UN conferences we will participate in throughout the year. High school level and university level conferences we will attend include: Nepean MUN, Lisgar MUN, Woodroffe MUN, CANIMUN, and SOMA.
